OUR PROJECT


The communities targeted to empower in this survey are UCLA students from any financial background, who have previously or currently held a job on campus in order to support themselves. This project is meant to shed light on this issue and make the UCLA community more aware of the problems caused by balancing a job and academics on working students. We hope that student workers at UCLA utilize this website to find others in a similar situation and feel a sense of community, and that others can view the site and listen to the narratives of student workers at UCLA.

More Information

Students surveyed

Students here at UCLA get jobs for many different reasons, as evidenced by clicking through the interactive map. The goal of our website is to create a community for student workers at UCLA to share their stories and struggles, even though financial situations are usually a stigmatized subject not often discussed. Since our website is completely anonymous, we hope that students feel comfortable sharing their stories. Contact us here with any suggestions or feedback.

Future uses

We hope that this website can be utilized in the future as a database/library for student worker unions to supplement their activism and fight for better benefits of student workers, and even work towards better financial aid/grant offers. Students are often pressured to work part-time jobs to relieve the financial burden of their family, which causes stress and takes away time from them for studies and social life. We hope that not only the student worker community at UCLA sees this website, but also others at UCLA will learn more about their peers.
